Diabetes Disease

Precautions

  • Avoid consuming sugary foods and beverages.
  • Follow a balanced diet and maintain a healthy weight.
  • Engage in regular physical activity.
  • Monitor blood sugar levels regularly.
  • Take prescribed medications as directed by your healthcare provider.
  • Keep stress levels under control.

Primary Causing Effects

Diabetes is primarily caused by a combination of genetic and lifestyle factors. Some of the main causing effects include:

  • Family history of diabetes
  • Obesity or overweight
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Poor diet, especially high intake of sugary and processed foods
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol levels

Side Effects

Diabetes can lead to various side effects and complications if not properly managed. Some common side effects include:

  • Increased risk of heart disease
  • Kidney damage
  • Nerve damage (neuropathy)
  • Eye problems and potential vision loss
  • Foot problems, including ulcers and infections
  • Increased susceptibility to infections

Cautions

  • Consult with your healthcare provider for proper diagnosis and treatment.
  • Follow a personalized diabetes management plan.
  • Be consistent with medication and insulin use.
  • Maintain a healthy diet and exercise routine.
  • Regularly monitor blood sugar levels and adjust treatment accordingly.
  • Seek immediate medical attention in case of severe symptoms or emergencies.
